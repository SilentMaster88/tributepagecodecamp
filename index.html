The Unknown Soldier

The man, the boy, the husband, the son, the grandson who never returned home

Here's a time line of of the monument to the unknown soldier:

  • 17 July 1920 The "Garibaldi, Society of Veterans of the Homeland Battles" and the "UNUS" (National Union of Officers and Soldiers) approved the proposal of Colonel Giulio Douhet for the burial in the Pantheon of an unrecognized soldier who fell during the First World War.
  • 20 June 1921 The bill for the "Burial of the body of an unknown soldier" was presented to the Chamber of Deputies
  • 28 June 1921 the 4th of November is declared a public holiday "dedicated to the celebration of honors to the unknown soldier"
  • October 1921 In October the commission identified the bodies of the eleven soldiers in different locations, trying to include places on the Italian front where the various weapons had fought, including the Regia Marina.
  • 28 October 1921 According to the instructions of the ministry, the eleven coffins, identical in shape and size, were gathered in the basilica of Aquileia
  • 28 October 1921 Also on 28 October at the Aquileia station the coffin was placed on a railway wagon with cannon carriage, specially designed by Guido Cirilli. On one side were written the dates mcmxv - mcmxviii; on the opposite side was the Dante quote, the shadow of him returns after his departure.
  • 29 October 1921 The special train left the next morning at 8 am. In addition to the wagon with the coffin, there were 15 wagons to collect the wreaths on the way; other first and second class carriages were destined for the escort of honor. The train stopped five minutes at each station on the route. The Ministry of War ordered the most rigorous silence during the passage of the train; public speeches were forbidden and on arrival of the train the song of the Piave could possibly be played only once. During the intermediate night stops (Venice, Bologna, Arezzo) the change was arranged for the representatives of senators, deputies, mothers, widows, mutilated persons and ex-combatants.
  • 2 November 2021 On the morning of November 2, when the coffin of the Unknown Soldier arrived at the Roma Termini station, it was greeted by the king and the royal family, by flags, banners and banners of the Army, Navy and Guardia di Finanza, with generals, commanders Army, Army and Navy Chiefs of Staff; they were invited, together with the various offices of the state, decorated with gold medals and representatives of the mutilated, mothers and widows of fallen and ex-combatants
  • 4 November 2021 On November 4, the third anniversary of the end of the war, at 8:30 the coffin was loaded onto a gun carriage. The long procession of the various weapons of the Army, Navy, Finance Police and Public Security Guard preceded the wagon, followed in turn by ten mothers and ten widows of the fallen, by representatives of State and Army positions and by representatives of mutilated and ex-combatants

"Worthy son of a brave lineage and a millenary civilization, he resisted inflexible in the most disputed trenches, lavished his courage in the most bloody battles and fell fighting without a doubt reward to hope that victory and the greatness of the fatherland."

-- Decree of 1 December 1921
